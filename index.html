Mutton Biryani Recipe

  • 500g Mutton (bone-in)
  • 2 cups Basmati Rice
  • 1 cup Yogurt
  • 2 Onions (thinly sliced)
  • 2 Tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • Whole Spices (Bay Leaf, Cloves, Cinnamon)
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh Coriander & Mint Leaves
  • Saffron soaked in warm milk
  • Ghee & Oil for cooking

Royal Mutton Biryani

A rich and aromatic rice dish made with tender mutton, fragrant basmati rice, and a perfect blend of traditional spices, slow-cooked to perfection.

  1. Marinate the mutton with yogurt, chili powder, turmeric, salt, and ginger-garlic paste. Let it sit for at least 2 hours.
  2. Fry the onions in ghee/oil until golden brown. Set half aside for later.
  3. Add chopped tomatoes to the remaining onions and cook until soft.
  4. Add marinated mutton and cook for 30‚Äì40 minutes or until tender.
  5. Parboil the basmati rice with salt and whole spices. Drain and keep aside.
  6. In a heavy-bottomed pot, layer mutton, rice, fried onions, mint, coriander, and saffron milk.
  7. Seal the pot with dough or a tight lid and cook on low flame (dum) for 20‚Äì25 minutes.
  8. Let it rest for 10 minutes before serving. Fluff gently and serve hot with raita or salad.
